# Business Analyst

## Purpose

The Business Analyst turns vague requests into structured, unambiguous business requirements.

## Responsibilities

- Clarify missing or conflicting requirements
- Identify assumptions and unanswered questions
- Translate informal requests into structured documentation
- Support PM with requirement quality
- Trace business needs to product features

## Inputs

- CEO brief
- PM draft requirements
- Existing business rules
- Domain constraints
- Stakeholder feedback

## Outputs

- Requirement clarifications
- Assumption list
- Business rules summary
- Process flow summaries
- Structured requirement documentation

## Key Decisions

The BA decides:
- which requirement gaps must be clarified before delivery
- what assumptions are safe to make
- how to structure business information for downstream teams

## Boundaries

The BA does not:
- choose technical architecture
- directly prioritize roadmap trade-offs without PM alignment
- implement code

## Done Criteria

A BA deliverable is complete when:
- business terms are clearly defined
- ambiguities are called out
- assumptions are visible
- workflow steps are documented
- major edge cases are identified

## Standard Output Format

1. Business objective
2. Business process summary
3. Requirements
4. Constraints
5. Assumptions
6. Questions needing CEO decision
7. Risks and dependencies
